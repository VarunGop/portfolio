Intro

Varun is currently a first year medical student at the Carle Illinois College of Medicine. He graduated summa cum laude from the University of Massachusetts Amherst with a degree in Biomedical Engineering. During this time he worked as an Emergency Medical Technician and Emergency Department Technician, where he fostered a passion for medical innovation to better patient outcomes. His work volunteering at the Amherst Survival Center food pantry additionally inspires him to focus on innovations to increase the accessibility of healthcare for all.

Varun is currently working on multiple projects exploring how innovations in healthcare can be used to both improve patient outcomes and reduce healthcare costs.

In his free time, Varun enjoys making music and spending time with his friends.

Projects

Biomedical Recognizer of Anatomy and Endotracheal Tube Holder (BRAETH)

BRAETH was created by a six person team led by Varun with fellow UMass Amherst biomedical engineering students. Under the supervision of Baystate Medical Center trauma surgeon, Dr. Tovy Kamine, Varun and the team developed a low-cost 3D printed device to assist providers with the performance of cricothyrotomies, a life-saving procedure for patients who are unable to breathe through their mouth or nose.

BRAETH performed extremely well in preliminary testing, reducing the time for providers to quickly perform a cricothyrotomy, and increasing their confidence with the procedure. Varun and the team are in the process of filing a provisional patent for BRAETH, and are currently testing the device in larger studies, with the aim of filing for a full patent in the near future.

ProjectEHR

Varun is currently working on a project with fellow medical students to develop a fully web based electronic health record system that can be used by medical students in their early training to increase their familiarity with EHR systems. Varun has mainly focused on the frontend development, making the UI as similar to current EHR systems as possible.

Customized Machine Learning Facial Emotion Recognition Algorithm

For his honors thesis project, Varun individually developed a machine learning algorithm trained on custom pictures of a singular subject to recognize human emotions from facial expressions. He tested this model against one trained on a generic set of data, like those currently used in facial emotion detection algorithms.

When tested on subjects, the custom model was able to perform on par with the generic model in the detection of happiness and sadness, but significantly outperformed the generic model in detecion of anger. This project reveals a potential mechanism for the emotional state monitoring of patients, especially those who express emotions in unconventional ways, allowing medical providers a noninvasive way to monitor everyone's emotions. Varun preseneted his work at the Massachusetts Undergraduate Research Conference.
